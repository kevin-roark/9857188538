I wrote a computer program called (985) 718-8538 that takes a sequence of valid dial-pad characters as input and processes them into a sequence of visualized DTMF tones and letters. Every button on a phone maps to a unique set of sound frequencies that you hear as you dial; every number or letter is represented as that sound. These sounds are identical on every phone and computerized telephone systems use this standard to talk to you. Passing words through (985) 718-8538 creates output that both computer phones and we can interpret. This is like computer "kissin me thru the phone."
